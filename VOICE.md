# VOICE

## Overview
Defines how content is generated, structured, and communicated across all outputs.

Ensures clarity, consistency, and effectiveness in messaging.

---

## 1. TONE ATTRIBUTES

- Clear  
- Confident  
- Human  
- Direct  

---

## 2. WRITING RULES

- Use short sentences  
- Use active voice only  
- Avoid jargon unless necessary  
- Avoid filler words  
- Prefer clarity over cleverness  

---

## 3. SENTENCE CONSTRUCTION (NEW)

- Max sentence length: 20 words  
- Prefer verbs over adjectives  
- Avoid vague phrasing  
- Avoid passive voice  

---

## 4. MESSAGING STRUCTURE (CRITICAL)

All outputs must follow:

1. Context  
2. Problem  
3. Solution  
4. Outcome  

---

## 5. PARAGRAPH RULES

- One idea per paragraph  
- No unnecessary transitions  
- Keep paragraphs concise  
- Avoid repetition  

---

## 6. AUDIENCE ADAPTATION

### B2B
- Structured  
- Data-driven  
- Professional  

### B2C
- Conversational  
- Relatable  
- Benefit-focused  

---

## 7. MODE SWITCHING (NEW)

### Deck Mode
- Short sentences  
- Bullet-heavy  
- Minimal text  

---

### Report Mode
- Structured paragraphs  
- More detailed explanations  
- Data-focused  

---

### Landing Page Mode
- Persuasive tone  
- Clear CTA  
- Scannable sections  

---

## 8. REWRITE RULES (IMPORTANT)

Weak → Strong

"We aim to leverage innovative solutions"  
→ "We solve X by doing Y"

"Leverage AI"  
→ "Use AI to achieve X"

"Enhance user experience"  
→ "Make X easier to use"

"Provide value"  
→ "Help users achieve X"

---

## 9. LANGUAGE STYLE RULES

- Be specific, not generic  
- Avoid abstract language  
- Avoid marketing clichés  
- Avoid exaggeration  
- Use concrete outcomes  

---

## 10. CONSISTENCY RULES

- Tone must remain consistent across all sections  
- Sentence style must not shift mid-document  
- Messaging must follow the same structure  
- Voice must not change between outputs  

---

## 11. ENFORCEMENT RULES (NEW)

- Reject vague or generic statements  
- Rewrite unclear sentences  
- Ensure messaging structure is followed  
- Ensure tone matches defined attributes  

---

## 12. OUTPUT VALIDATION

Before publishing, verify:

- Tone is consistent  
- Sentences are clear and concise  
- Messaging follows defined structure  
- No filler or vague language  

If validation fails:
→ Output must be revised  

---

## 13. FAILURE CONDITIONS

An output fails if:

- Tone is inconsistent  
- Sentences are too long or unclear  
- Messaging lacks structure  
- Content is vague or generic  
- Language becomes overly complex  

---

## FINAL PRINCIPLE

If language is unclear, the message fails.

Clarity is the system.
